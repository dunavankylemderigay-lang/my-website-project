Digital Ethics and Faith

“Do to others as you would have them do to you.” — Luke 6:31

Living with Integrity Online

As technology becomes part of our daily lives, our faith must guide how we act, speak, and share online. Digital ethics means showing respect, honesty, and kindness even when no one is watching — because God always is.

1. Be Honest and Truthful

Avoid plagiarism, false information, or deception online. Integrity means being truthful in what we post, share, or create.

2. Show Kindness and Respect

Treat others with love and compassion, even during disagreements. Our words should reflect God’s love and not hatred or mockery.

3. Protect Privacy and Dignity

Be careful about sharing personal information and photos — your own or others’. Respect boundaries and promote safety.

4. Use Technology to Uplift

Share content that inspires hope, spreads truth, and uplifts spirits. Be a light in the digital world by using your voice for good.

Reflection

Practicing digital ethics is an act of faith — a way to honor God in our online behavior. By putting “Una ang Ginoo” first, we ensure that our actions reflect His character and bring light to the digital space.
